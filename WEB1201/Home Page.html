CAUSES
Causes of Climate Change

Deforestation

Cutting down forests for farms, pastures, or other purposes releases carbon from trees. Since trees absorb carbon dioxide, destroying the trees in forests reduces nature's ability to absorb carbon dioxide.

Most vehicles use fossil fuels, which means transportation emits greenhouse gases, like carbon dioxide. Although road vehicles emit the most, but ship and plane emissions continue to grow.

Transportation

Manufacturing

Manufacturing and industry emit largely from burning fossil fuels to make cement, iron, steel, electronics, plastics, textiles, and other items. Industrial processes like mining also release gases.

EFFECTS
Effects of Climate Change
Food Shortages
Climate change and extreme weather events contribute to world hunger and malnutrition. Fisheries, crops, and animals may be lost or reduced. Heat stress reduces water and grazing areas.
Rising Sea Levels
Most of the heat from global warming is absorbed by the ocean. This threatens coastal and island communities by melting ice sheets and raising sea levels. Carbon dioxide is also absorbed by the water, keeping it out of the atmosphere. The ocean becomes more acidic as a result of increased carbon dioxide, endangering marine life.
Loss of Species
Both animals on land and in the ocean are at risk from climate change. As the temperatures rise, these risks rise as well. Among the various dangers include forest fires, severe weather, invasive pests, and diseases. Some species may be able to relocate and live on, but some species will not be able to.
ACTIONS
Actions You Can Take To Help

Use Public Transport

Most diesel and petrol automobiles choke the world's roads. Walking or biking instead of driving reduces greenhouse gas emissions and improves fitness. Take a train or bus for longer trips, and carpool when possible.

Eat Vegetables

Eating more vegetables, fruits, whole grains, legumes, nuts, and seeds and less meat and dairy can drastically reduce your environmental impact. Plant-based diets produce less greenhouse gas emissions, energy, land, and water.

Save Energy

Coal, oil, and gas provide our electricity and heat. Lower your heating and cooling, use LED light bulbs and energy-efficient appliances, wash your clothes in cold water, and hang them to dry to save energy.
